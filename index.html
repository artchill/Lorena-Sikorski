About the Book

A memoir of survival
and self-discovery.

Only later in life did Lorena Sikorski come to understand the name for what she had endured: her mother suffered from narcissistic personality disorder. Artists and Vagabonds traces a childhood of conditional love, physical abuse, and constant relocations — a portrait of the devastating environment shaped by an abusive mother and an inept father who could not, or would not, intervene.

With unflinching honesty, Sikorski recounts her journey through survival and into adulthood, including the parallel discovery of who she truly is — a lesbian — and the hard-won freedom that followed. It is a story about the long road back to oneself.

About the Author

Lorena L. Sikorski

Ms. Sikorski is a retired music educator who taught music and science in the Garden Grove Unified School District in Orange County, California, for thirty-five years. She is a professional musician, performing on violin and trombone in many groups, and now runs her own music studio.

She holds a bachelor of arts degree in music ('73) and a master of science degree in education ('84) from California State University–Fullerton.
